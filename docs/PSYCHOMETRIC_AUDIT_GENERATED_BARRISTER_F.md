**Overall exam quality:** Generated Barrister F is a strong exam by the standards of AI-generated question banks. The PASS rate of 79% (126/160 clear questions) reflects a well-calibrated pool. The IMPROVE rate of 18% (28/160) is acceptable and concentrated in a predictable pattern: questions that test the *name* of a rule rather than its *application to specific facts*. - Family Law is the highest-quality subject block. The child protection, child support retroactivity, and common-law property questions are at genuine LSO exam standard. The 28 IMPROVE questions share a common weakness: the scenario and call are satisfied by rule recall without applying the rule to distinguishing facts. This pattern is most visible in civil litigation (procedural definitions — civ-03-soc-form-001, civ-12-throwaway-001) and public law (constitutional definitions — pub-02-s3-001, pub-02-s6-001). The fix in each case is the same: add one or two constraining facts that create a genuine choice between plausible options.

**Distractor quality:**  
The dominant distractor weakness across the bank is inclusion of at least one obviously-wrong option per question (visible particularly in IMPROVE-rated questions). Strong questions (PASS at realism=5) consistently have all three wrong answers trap-engineered with identifiable trap types. Questions that score distractor=3 typically have two strong distractors and one "throwaway" that serves no discriminating function.

**Rationale quality:**  
Across all 160 questions, the `why_X_wrong` field pattern is good: most questions identify the correct answer and explain each distractor's error. The weakness in IMPROVE-rated questions is that the rationale dismisses distractors without naming the trap type. For PASS questions where rationale=4 (most of the bank), the rationale is functionally adequate but could be upgraded by explicitly labelling each distractor trap using the taxonomy in the rubric.

**Prose cleanliness:**  
Prose quality is uniformly high across the bank. No question scored below 4 on prose. The questions are free of double negatives, bloated facts, and vague pronouns. This is one of the strongest psychometric features of the Generated Barrister F pool.

**Ethics integration:**  
Only four questions in exam F have explicit professional responsibility angles: fam-08-cyfsa-def-001 (mandatory reporting overrides discretion — well-executed), civ-12-repres-001, civ-16-counsel-001, and fam-08-duty-rep-002 (not in exam F but in the source file).
