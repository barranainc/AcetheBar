## Exam-Level Summary

### Strengths

1. **Criminal law questions are consistently strong.** The search warrant series, Grant test questions, voir dire questions, defences (duress, intoxication-to-manslaughter), and YCJA advanced questions all demonstrate high application-over-lookup scores and well-engineered traps.

2. **Family law variation and domestic contracts sets are standout material.** The spousal support variation questions (fam-05-variation-001 through -004), the FLA s.56(4) setting-aside questions, and the FRO opt-out question are among the best in the bank.

3. **Public law bias and SPPA questions are excellent.** The Valente administrative independence question (pub-03-bias-003) and the SPPA reconsideration question (pub-05-sppa-rec-001) are exam-trap quality and demonstrate the kind of nuanced statutory precision the LSO exam requires.

4. **POGG, paramountcy, and IJI questions in public law are all realism-5.** These three constitutional questions are among the hardest and best-calibrated questions in the exam.

### Weaknesses

1. **Easy-difficulty questions tend to have thin distractors.** Seven of the ten REWRITE recommendations involve easy questions where the wrong answers are too obviously wrong. The fix is consistent: introduce a borderline application scenario rather than a simple rule-recall question.

2. **Criminal law ch09 sentencing and ch10 YCJA easy questions need rebuilding.** crim-09-purpose-001 and crim-10-ycja-def-001 test list-recall rather than statutory application and should be redesigned.

3. **Civil litigation ch03, ch04, ch08 weak distractors.** Three civil litigation questions need distractor engineering upgrades.
