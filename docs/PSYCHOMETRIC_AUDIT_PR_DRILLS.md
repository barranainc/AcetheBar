## PR-Specific Quality Observations

### Strengths

**1. Practical judgment is consistently tested.** The vast majority of questions do not ask "what does Rule X say?" — they ask "what should the lawyer do in this specific situation?" The five PR judgment categories identified in the audit mandate (what to do next; when to withdraw; when to breach confidentiality; when is consent required/insufficient; how to deal with represented/unrepresented parties) are all well represented and usually treated with genuine nuance.

**2. Ethics integration is the bank's outstanding characteristic.** Almost every question in chapters 3, 4, 7, 9, and 10 contains at least one Ethics Override Trap or Pragmatic Bluff distractor — the options that are commercially attractive or seem helpful to the client but are professionally impermissible. This is exactly what the LSO exam tests and the bank delivers it reliably.

**3. Exam-trap questions (difficulty = "exam_trap") are among the best in the bank.** The joint retainer questions (pr-04-joint-ret-002 through -005), the trust withdrawal question (pr-06-trust-004), the crime-fraud exception questions (pr-03-confid-excep-004), and the mandatory withdrawal question (pr-09-mand-001) are all 5/5 on realism. They are indistinguishable from actual LSO exam material.

**4. Rationale quality is excellent throughout.** With very few exceptions, the explanations identify why the correct answer is right, name the trap in each distractor, and explain why the fact pattern defeats each wrong option. This is pedagogically strong and supports self-assessment by candidates.

**5. Prose is clean and tight.** No question has a double-negative call, ambiguous pronoun, or bloated fact pattern. Reading time estimates appear realistic across difficulty levels.

### Weaknesses

**1. Distractor weakness in a subset of questions.** The primary weakness in the bank is concentrated in approximately 15 questions where one or more wrong answers are obviously incorrect to any prepared candidate. This is most common in ch01, ch12, and ch13 — the regulatory/administrative chapters where the rule is more mechanical. These questions need at least one additional trap-engineered distractor.

**2. Overlap among related questions on the same subtopic.** Three pairs of questions cover essentially the same rule from essentially the same angle: pr-03-confid-scope-001 and pr-03-post-ret-001 (both test permanence of confidentiality); pr-06-short-001 through -003 (the third shortfall question adds little); pr-11-penalty-001 and -002 (both test Tribunal penalties without meaningful differentiation). In each case, the weaker question in the pair should be redesigned to test a distinct aspect of the same topic area.

**3. Trust accounting questions (ch06) are competent but light on ethics integration.** By-Law 9 questions inevitably trend toward statutory precision rather than ethical override. The questions correctly identify and test the By-Law obligations, but most score 3–4 on ethics integration rather than 5 because the ethical override element (e.g., client pressure, client instructions to misuse trust funds) is not foregrounded. The bank should include at least two additional questions where the candidate must choose between a client instruction and a trust accounting obligation — these are the highest-value PR exam questions in the trust accounting chapter.

**4. Government lawyer questions (ch03) need more specificity.** The LSO Barrister exam recognizes that government lawyers face distinctive confidentiality and conflict obligations (the "client" is the Crown, specific ministries, public servants or the institution). Only one question (pr-03-govt-001) addresses this directly and it is the weakest in the chapter. This is a high-frequency exam topic that deserves at least two well-constructed questions, including one on the government lawyer's obligation when instructed by a minister to take a position the lawyer considers legally incorrect.

**5. Limited-scope retainer coverage is good but could include one more high-difficulty question.** pr-02-scope-002 (the "unreasonably narrow scope" question) is excellent. However, the bank would benefit from one additional question testing the intersection of limited-scope retainers and the duty to advise on urgent adjacent matters discovered during the limited retainer — a real-world scenario that tests whether scope limitations override the lawyer's duty to flag urgencies.
